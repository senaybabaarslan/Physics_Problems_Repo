## Problem 10 – Angular Momentum (Spinning)

Spinning in a circle: $\vec r = (R\cos \omega t, R\sin \omega t, 0)$.
1. **Angular Momentum ($\vec L$):** This is the "strength" of the spin.
   $\vec L = m(\vec r \times \vec v) = \mathbf{(0, 0, mR^2\omega)}$
2. **Meaning:** The spin is constant and points straight up (Z-axis). It follows the **Right-Hand Rule** (curl your fingers, thumb points up!).
