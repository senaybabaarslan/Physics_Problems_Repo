## Problem 10: Angular Momentum in Circles

**Given:** $\vec r(t) = (R\cos \omega t, R\sin \omega t, 0)$
1. **Velocity:** $\vec v(t) = (-R\omega \sin \omega t, R\omega \cos \omega t, 0)$
2. **Angular Momentum ($\vec L$):** $m(\vec r \times \vec v)$
   $$\vec L = m \begin{vmatrix} \mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\ R\cos\omega t & R\sin\omega t & 0 \\ -R\omega\sin\omega t & R\omega\cos\omega t & 0 \end{vmatrix} = \mathbf{(0, 0, mR^2\omega)}$$
3. **Properties:** $|\vec L| = mR^2\omega$ is a constant. The vector points in the Z-direction, which is perpendicular to the motion.
4. **Right-Hand Rule:** If the object rotates in the $xy$ plane, your thumb (Angular Momentum) points along the $z$ axis.
5. **Torque Relationship:** $\vec \tau = \frac{d\vec L}{dt}$. Since $\vec L$ is constant, $\frac{d\vec L}{dt} = 0$. This means the torque $\vec \tau = \mathbf{0}$ for uniform circular motion.
