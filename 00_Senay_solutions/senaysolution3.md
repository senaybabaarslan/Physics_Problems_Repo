## Problem 3 – Integration (Going Backwards)

### Part A: We have velocity, find position.
$\vec v(t) = (2t, 3, -e^{-t})$ and we start at $(0, 1, 2)$.
* Integrate $2t \to t^2$
* Integrate $3 \to 3t$
* Integrate $-e^{-t} \to e^{-t}$
* Add the starting point: $\vec r(t) = \mathbf{(t^2, 3t+1, e^{-t}+1)}$
