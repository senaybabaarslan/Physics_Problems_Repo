## Problem 5 – Trajectory Curvature

**Ellipse:** $x = a\cos t, y = b\sin t$

1. $\vec v = (-a\sin t, b\cos t)$, $\vec a = (-a\cos t, -b\sin t)$
2. $a_n(t) = \frac{|\vec v \times \vec a|}{|\vec v|} = \frac{ab}{\sqrt{a^2\sin^2 t + b^2\cos^2 t}}$
3. **Radius of Curvature at $t=0$:**
   $$R = \frac{v^2}{a_n} = \frac{b^2}{a}$$
4. **Interpretation:** Curvature is highest at the end of the major axis. Normal acceleration changes only the direction of motion.
