## Problem 5: Ellipse Curvature and Physics

**Trajectory:** $x = a\cos t, y = b\sin t$
* **Velocity:** $\vec v = (-a\sin t, b\cos t)$
* **Acceleration:** $\vec a = (-a\cos t, -b\sin t)$

**Normal Acceleration ($a_n$):** This is the part of acceleration that makes the object "turn".
* $a_n = \frac{|\vec v \times \vec a|}{|\vec v|} = \frac{ab}{\sqrt{a^2\sin^2 t + b^2\cos^2 t}}$

**Radius of Curvature ($R$) at $t=0$:**
At $t=0$, speed $v = b$ and $a_n = a$.
Using $a_n = v^2 / R$:
$a = b^2 / R \implies \mathbf{R = b^2/a}$

**Physical Interpretation:**
* **More Curvature?** Yes, the more the path "bends", the higher the normal acceleration needed.
* **Where is it most "curved"?** At the ends of the **major axis** (the long tips of the egg shape).
* **Why Normal Acceleration?** It pushes perpendicular to the motion. It doesn't change speed; it only changes the **direction**.
